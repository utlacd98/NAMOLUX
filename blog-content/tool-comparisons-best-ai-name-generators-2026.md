## 1. NamoLux — Best for Scored, Data-Backed Decisions

**What it does:** Generates brandable names from a keyword brief, checks availability across .com, .io, .ai, and .co in real-time, and scores every name with Founder Signal™ — a 0–100 rating covering phonetics, memorability, brand risk, and domain quality.

**Best for:** Founders who want to make a naming decision with objective quality data rather than pure aesthetic judgment.

**Pricing:** One-time purchase. One free generation per day, no account required.

**Honest take:** The scoring system is what separates NamoLux from everything else on this list. You're not just browsing names — you're getting structured feedback on why a name works or doesn't. The logo preview feature that Namelix has is absent here, which some founders will miss.

## 2. Namelix — Best Free Option for Visual Exploration

**What it does:** Generates names from keywords with style controls (short, compound, real word, made-up word), and shows you simple logo mockups alongside each name.

**Best for:** Early ideation when you want to explore many directions quickly and see rough visual concepts.

**Pricing:** Free with ads. Premium tier for logo creation.

**Honest take:** The best free name generator by a significant margin. Logo previews are genuinely useful for non-designers who need to visualise how a name might look as a brand. No domain checking depth and no quality scoring keeps it in the "ideation" category rather than "decision-making" category.

## 3. Looka — Best for Founders Who Want Name + Logo in One Session

**What it does:** Primarily a logo generator that also includes name suggestions. You can get a brand package with name, logo, and business card templates.

**Best for:** Founders who want to move quickly from name to visual identity.

**Pricing:** Free for exploration, paid for downloads (£65–£100 one-time).

**Honest take:** Name quality is inconsistent — Looka's strength is logo generation, and names feel like a secondary feature. If visual brand assets are your priority and name selection is less critical, the combined package has good value.

## 4. Shopify Business Name Generator — Best for E-Commerce

**What it does:** Simple keyword-to-name tool, checks .com availability immediately, shows which Shopify store names are available.

**Best for:** E-commerce founders who want a name and store setup simultaneously.

**Pricing:** Free.

**Honest take:** The output is basic and heavily weighted toward compound-keyword names that tend to be generic. The .com-only checking and Shopify-specific workflow makes it niche. Worth a five-minute try if you're building on Shopify, but not for serious brand exploration.

## 5. Squadhelp — Best for High-Stakes Naming with Human Creativity

**What it does:** A crowdsourced naming platform where you post a brief, real humans compete to submit name ideas, and you pick a winner. Includes trademark screening on the winning name.

**Best for:** Funded companies that want a large volume of human-generated options and don't mind a 7–10 day timeline.

**Pricing:** Contest fees start around £150, rise to £500+ for premium contests. Trademark screening is additional.

**Honest take:** The quality ceiling is higher than pure AI tools because human namers bring creativity and cultural context that algorithms miss. The volume of submissions (often 100–300 per contest) requires significant evaluation work. Not suited for founders who need a name in a day.

## 6. BrandBucket — Best for Pre-Built Premium Domains

**What it does:** A marketplace of pre-built brandable domains, each designed and priced as a brand asset. You're not generating names — you're buying one that's already been created, vetted, and paired with a logo.

**Best for:** Founders with budget and a preference for something truly unique.

**Pricing:** Individual domains typically £1,500–£5,000. Occasionally more for premium names.

**Honest take:** The quality is excellent. BrandBucket's curation is strict — these are genuinely strong brandable names. The cost is the barrier. For a funded startup where the name is a strategic asset, the investment can make sense. For bootstrappers, the ROI is hard to justify.

## 7. Namecheap Generator — Best Paired with Registration

**What it does:** Enter a keyword, get available domain suggestions across multiple TLDs, immediately register anything you like.

**Best for:** Founders who already know approximately what name they want and need to check availability.

**Pricing:** Free to search, standard registration costs per domain.

**Honest take:** Not really a "name generator" in the creative sense — it's a domain availability checker with mild name variation features. Useful at the end of a naming process when you're finalising a decision, not at the beginning.

💡 **The two-tool approach:** Many founders get the best results using two tools in sequence. Namelix for broad creative ideation (it's free, it's fast, it shows visual concepts). NamoLux for scoring and final selection (Founder Signal™ tells you which names are actually worth committing to). The combination covers both the generative and evaluative phases.

## Overall Verdict

For most founders naming an early-stage startup or SaaS:

**Free tier, maximum speed:** Namelix or NamoLux (one free generation)
**Scored decision-making:** NamoLux
**Human creativity, budget available:** Squadhelp
**Premium pre-built brand:** BrandBucket
**Already know the name, just need the domain:** Namecheap

The single biggest mistake is choosing a name based purely on aesthetics and availability. A name that scores well on brand quality criteria — phonetics, memorability, uniqueness, domain strength — will outperform an aesthetically pleasing but structurally weak name over the long run.
